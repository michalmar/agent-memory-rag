## 1. Purpose

This directive governs access to and use of the company's internal canteen. It
supports affordable meals for employees, safe dietary choices, inclusive shift
coverage, and reduced packaging and food waste.

## 2. Scope

This directive applies to employees, interns, on-site contractors, and
authorized visitors using a Northstar Solutions canteen. It covers meal
eligibility, ordering, payment, conduct, allergens, takeaway meals, and
sustainability requirements.

## 3. Opening hours and service

On normal working days, the canteen provides:

- breakfast from 07:30 to 10:00;
- lunch from 11:00 to 14:30; and
- refrigerated late-shift meals from 14:30 to 20:00.

At lunch, the standard service includes at least two hot main meals, with one
vegetarian and one plant-based choice, plus soup and salad. Workplace Services
may adjust hours for site closures, maintenance, or exceptional occupancy and
will announce planned changes in advance.

## 4. Eligibility and subsidy

Employees and interns receive a 65 percent company subsidy, up to EUR 7.50 per
scheduled working day, toward one qualifying meal. A qualifying meal is a
breakfast set, standard lunch, or late-shift meal.

The subsidy:

- is linked to the individual's employee badge or canteen-app account;
- may be used on an on-site working day, including an approved partial remote
  working day;
- cannot be transferred, combined across days, or exchanged for cash; and
- excludes alcohol, confectionery, premium bottled drinks, and retail goods.

On-site contractors pay the full listed price unless their service agreement
provides a meal benefit. Authorized visitors pay the full price.

## 5. Ordering and payment

Employees may buy available meals at the counter or preorder lunch through the
canteen app before 10:00. A preorder is held until 13:30.

An employee who repeatedly fails to collect reserved meals may lose preorder
access for 30 days after two reminders. A charge may be applied only when the
app clearly disclosed the charge before confirmation.

The employee portion may be paid by debit card, mobile wallet, or payroll
deduction where available. Payment errors must be raised within ten working
days.

## 6. Menu, nutrition, and allergens

Menus and ingredient information are published in the canteen app and at the
service counter. The caterer identifies legally regulated allergens and labels
vegetarian and plant-based meals. Nutritional information is provided where
reliable recipe data is available.

Separate utensils and preparation procedures are used for designated
allergen-controlled meals, but the shared kitchen cannot guarantee an
allergen-free environment. An employee with a severe allergy should contact
the canteen manager for a documented ingredient and cross-contact review before
ordering.

Suspected incorrect allergen information must be reported immediately. The
affected dish must be withdrawn until the canteen manager completes a check.

## 7. Takeaway meals and reusable containers

Meals may be taken away in:

- a company reusable container issued under the deposit scheme; or
- a clean personal container accepted by canteen staff as suitable for the
  selected food.

Single-use takeaway containers are available only when required for hygiene or
business continuity and may carry a packaging charge. A subsidized takeaway
meal is for the employee's own consumption and must not be resold.

Chilled and hot food must be stored and consumed according to the label.
Northstar Solutions and the caterer are not responsible for unsafe storage or
reheating after a meal leaves the controlled service area.

## 8. Food waste and sustainability

Users should preorder when possible and select a portion they expect to eat.
Unopened packaged food must be returned to the designated collection point
rather than left on tables.

Where food-safety rules permit, the caterer may discount surplus meals near the
end of service or transfer them to an approved food-donation partner.
Employees may not remove unoffered kitchen surplus.

All users must separate food waste, reusable items, and other waste using the
labeled collection stations.

## 9. Shared-space conduct

Canteen users must:

- follow queue and food-safety instructions from canteen staff;
- return trays, dishes, cutlery, and reusable containers;
- leave tables reasonably clean;
- avoid speakerphone calls and disruptive audio;
- keep emergency routes and accessible seating clear; and
- treat staff and other users respectfully.

Seats must not be reserved for more than 10 minutes during peak service.
Informal meetings are permitted outside the 11:45 to 13:30 peak period if they
do not prevent diners from finding a seat.

## 10. Guests, events, and shift needs

Departments expecting more than five visitors must notify Workplace Services
at least three working days in advance. Event catering must be ordered
separately and is not eligible for the employee subsidy.

Managers of evening or weekend teams must inform Workplace Services of expected
attendance by the weekly ordering deadline so an appropriate meal option can
be arranged.

## 11. Feedback, complaints, and incidents

Users may submit menu suggestions and service complaints through the Workplace
Services portal or canteen app. Suspected foodborne illness, foreign objects,
unsafe temperatures, or incorrect allergen information must be reported
immediately to the canteen manager and Workplace Services.

Workplace Services records and investigates food-safety complaints with the
caterer and escalates reportable incidents to the appropriate authority.

## 12. Privacy

The badge system and canteen app record meal eligibility, subsidy value,
payment status, and preorder collection. Individual meal choices are not
provided to managers or used for employment decisions.

Transaction records are retained for 24 months for payroll, tax, fraud
prevention, and audit purposes, unless local law requires a different period.

## 13. Misuse and exceptions

Badge sharing, subsidy fraud, abusive conduct, theft, resale of subsidized
meals, or unauthorized entry into kitchen areas may result in loss of canteen
access and disciplinary action. Exceptions require written approval from the
Head of Workplace Services.
