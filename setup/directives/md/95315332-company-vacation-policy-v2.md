## 1. Purpose

This directive establishes a fair and transparent process for earning,
requesting, using, and carrying over paid annual vacation. It supports employee
well-being, predictable team coverage, and equal access to popular leave
periods.

## 2. Scope

This directive applies to all employees. Entitlement for part-time employees is
prorated according to contracted working time. Contractors and agency workers
are not covered unless their contract states otherwise.

Statutory leave, collective agreements, and employment contracts take
precedence where they provide a different or greater entitlement.

## 3. Vacation year and entitlement

The vacation year runs from January 1 through December 31.

All full-time employees receive 25 working days of paid annual vacation per
year, regardless of length of service. Employees who start or leave during the
year receive a prorated entitlement. Vacation accrues monthly and is available
from the employee's start date.

In addition, each employee receives two paid personal well-being days per
calendar year. These days:

- may be requested on the day they are needed;
- do not require the employee to disclose a reason;
- cannot be carried over or paid out; and
- are separate from annual vacation and sick leave.

Public holidays and other statutory leave are not deducted from annual
vacation.

## 4. Requesting and approving vacation

Vacation must be requested in the HR system and approved before travel or other
commitments are made.

- Requests for one to four working days should be submitted at least five
  calendar days in advance.
- Requests for five to nine working days should be submitted at least 15
  calendar days in advance.
- Requests for ten or more working days should be submitted at least 30
  calendar days in advance.

Managers must normally approve, decline, or request alternate dates within five
working days. A decline must include an operational reason.

For school holidays and year-end periods, managers will consider team coverage,
prior-year access, caring responsibilities, and employee preferences rather
than using a first-come-first-served rule alone.

## 5. Planning and disconnection

Employees should submit an outline of their planned vacation by March 31 and
are encouraged to take at least one break of ten consecutive working days.
Every employee should take at least one break of five consecutive working days
unless prevented by protected leave or another exceptional circumstance.

Before leave, employees must arrange a reasonable handover and out-of-office
message. Employees are not expected to read messages, attend meetings, or
remain routinely available during vacation. Remote work from another location
is work and must not be recorded as vacation.

## 6. Carryover

Up to ten unused vacation days may be carried into the next vacation year with
manager approval. Carried days must be used by June 30 and are used before the
new year's entitlement.

Additional days may carry over when the employee could not take leave because
of certified long-term sickness, protected leave, a documented company
requirement, or applicable law. Managers and People Operations must prepare a
leave plan when an employee has more than 15 unused days on October 1.

## 7. Optional vacation purchase

Employees who have completed probation may request to purchase up to five
additional vacation days for the following year through the annual benefits
enrollment process. Approval depends on team capacity and local payroll rules.
The cost is deducted evenly from salary during that vacation year.

Purchased days:

- must be used after statutory and standard company vacation;
- cannot normally be carried over; and
- are refunded through payroll if employment ends before they can be used.

## 8. Changes and cancellation

Employees may change or cancel approved vacation with manager consent. The
company may cancel approved vacation only for a serious and unforeseen
business-continuity need, with approval from the business-unit director and
People Operations.

The business unit will reimburse reasonable, documented, non-refundable costs
directly caused by a company cancellation. A manager may not ask an employee to
cancel leave solely because routine work was not reassigned.

## 9. Sickness during vacation

Vacation days may be converted to sick leave when the employee promptly follows
the normal sickness-reporting process and provides evidence required by local
law or the Sickness Absence Directive. Converted vacation days are returned to
the employee's balance.

## 10. Pay and termination

Vacation is paid using the employee's normal vacation-pay calculation under
local law. The company does not pay cash instead of vacation during employment
except where legally required.

On termination, unused accrued vacation is paid or scheduled during the notice
period as permitted by law. Personal well-being days are not paid out.
Vacation taken beyond the accrued balance may be recovered where legally
permitted and authorized.

## 11. Responsibilities

Employees must keep leave records accurate and provide an appropriate handover.
Managers must apply the approval criteria consistently, monitor balances,
protect minimum coverage, and support genuine disconnection from work.

People Operations maintains balances, audits approval patterns, and advises on
statutory or exceptional cases.

## 12. Non-compliance and exceptions

Absence without approval or notification may be treated as unauthorized
absence. Exceptions require written approval from the People Operations
Director and must be recorded in the HR system.
