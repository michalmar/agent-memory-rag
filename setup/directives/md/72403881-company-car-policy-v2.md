## 1. Purpose

This directive defines the eligibility, assignment, safe use, environmental
standards, and return of company cars. It supports necessary business travel
while reducing fleet emissions and operating risk.

## 2. Scope

This directive applies to all employees who are assigned a company-owned or
company-leased passenger vehicle. Pool vehicles and short-term rentals are
governed by the Fleet Booking Procedure and Business Travel Directive.

Where local law, tax rules, an employment agreement, or a collective agreement
sets a different requirement, that requirement takes precedence.

## 3. Eligibility and annual review

A company car may be assigned when:

- the employee's role is expected to require at least 12,000 business
  kilometers per year;
- the employee travels for business on at least 40 percent of working days; or
- the Executive Committee has approved the car as part of the employee's
  compensation package.

The employee's manager, People Operations, and Fleet Management must approve
the assignment. Eligibility is reviewed each January using the prior year's
business mileage. Fleet Management may replace an underused car with access to
a pool vehicle or a mobility allowance.

## 4. Vehicle selection and replacement

Battery-electric vehicles are the default for all new assignments. Fleet
Management may approve a plug-in hybrid or efficient combustion vehicle when
documented route, charging, payload, or regional infrastructure requirements
make an electric vehicle impractical.

Vehicles must be selected from the approved catalog and role-based cost band.
They are normally replaced after 48 months, 140,000 kilometers, or when total
maintenance cost makes earlier replacement economical.

## 5. Permitted use

The vehicle may be used for company business, commuting, and private travel
where private use is included in the assignment letter. Private travel is
limited to 8,000 kilometers per calendar year unless a higher allowance is
approved as a taxable benefit.

Only the assigned employee may drive the vehicle. A properly licensed adult may
drive only in an emergency where the employee cannot continue safely.

The vehicle must not be used for racing, driver instruction, commercial
passenger or delivery services, unauthorized towing, off-road recreation, or
travel to a country excluded by the insurer.

## 6. Charging, fuel, tolls, and parking

For electric vehicles:

- public charging for business travel must use the company charging card;
- home charging is reimbursed at the published quarterly electricity rate
  based on verified vehicle data;
- the company may contribute up to EUR 900 toward one approved home charger
  installation; and
- charging costs for private travel are the employee's responsibility.

For other vehicles, the fuel card may be used only for business travel and
commuting. Business tolls and parking may be claimed through the expense
system.

Traffic fines, parking penalties, impound fees, and costs caused by negligent
misuse remain the employee's responsibility.

## 7. Driver safety responsibilities

The detailed controls for this section are defined in the
[Company Car Driver Safety Requirements (open PDF)](../pdf/30336958-company-car-driver-safety-requirements-v1.pdf),
Directive ID 30336958, version 1.0, file
`[30336958-company-car-driver-safety-requirements-v1.pdf]`. That sub-directive
forms part of this policy and is mandatory for every authorized driver.

The assigned employee must:

- maintain a valid license and report any suspension or restriction before
  driving;
- complete the company's defensive-driving course every two years;
- never drive while impaired or dangerously fatigued;
- not hold or operate a phone while the vehicle is moving;
- ensure all occupants use seat belts;
- keep the vehicle smoke-free and vape-free;
- complete the monthly digital safety inspection;
- arrange servicing, recalls, tires, and statutory inspections on time; and
- use winter tires where required by law or Fleet Management.

## 8. Damage, breakdown, and accidents

Accidents, theft, vandalism, and material damage must be reported through the
Fleet Portal within 12 hours, or immediately when a person is injured. The
driver must contact emergency services or the police when required by law, when
there is an injury, or when theft is suspected.

The driver must not admit liability or agree to a private settlement. Available
photographs, witness details, police references, and third-party information
must be uploaded to the incident record.

## 9. Insurance and driver charges

The company maintains fleet insurance for authorized use. The employee may be
charged the insurance excess for an at-fault incident where an investigation
identifies serious negligence, a prohibited use, or failure to report on time.
Any charge will follow local law and the Payroll Deduction Standard.

## 10. Vehicle data and privacy

Fleet vehicles use telematics to record odometer readings, charging or fuel
consumption, diagnostic alerts, location, and safety events such as harsh
braking. Data is used for maintenance, mileage validation, theft recovery,
incident investigation, and aggregated fleet planning.

Fleet Management does not use routine location data to assess employee
performance. Access is role-based, logged, and limited to a defined business
purpose. Detailed trip data is retained for 12 months unless an open
investigation or law requires longer retention.

## 11. Return or suspension of the vehicle

The vehicle must be returned on the date specified by Fleet Management when:

- employment ends;
- the employee moves to an ineligible role;
- business mileage remains below the eligibility threshold for a full review
  year;
- unpaid or extended leave is expected to exceed 60 days; or
- the assignment is suspended due to unsafe or prohibited use.

All keys, cards, charging cables, documents, and standard equipment must be
returned. The employee and Fleet Management will jointly record the vehicle's
condition.

## 12. Non-compliance and exceptions

Misuse may result in temporary suspension or withdrawal of the vehicle,
recovery of documented costs, and disciplinary action. Exceptions require a
written business justification and approval from the policy owner and the
business-unit director.
