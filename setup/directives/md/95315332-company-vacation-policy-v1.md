## 1. Purpose

This directive establishes a consistent process for earning, requesting, using,
and carrying over paid annual vacation. The company expects employees to take
regular time away from work for rest and recovery.

## 2. Scope

This directive applies to all employees. Entitlement for part-time employees is
prorated according to contracted working time. Contractors and agency workers
are not covered unless their contract states otherwise.

Statutory leave, collective agreements, and employment contracts take
precedence where they provide a different or greater entitlement.

## 3. Vacation year and entitlement

The vacation year runs from January 1 through December 31.

Full-time employees receive:

- 20 working days per year during their first four completed years of service;
  and
- 25 working days per year from the fifth service anniversary onward.

Employees who start or leave during the year receive a prorated entitlement.
Vacation accrues monthly and may be used after the first full month of
employment.

Public holidays, sick leave, parental leave, and other statutory leave are not
deducted from annual vacation.

## 4. Requesting vacation

Vacation must be requested in the HR system and approved before travel or other
commitments are made.

- Requests for one to four working days should be submitted at least five
  calendar days in advance.
- Requests for five to nine working days should be submitted at least ten
  calendar days in advance.
- Requests for ten or more working days should be submitted at least 30
  calendar days in advance.

Managers should respond within seven calendar days. Requests during popular
periods are normally considered in the order received while maintaining
minimum team coverage.

## 5. Planning and minimum leave

Employees should submit an outline of their planned vacation by March 31.
Managers may ask employees to adjust dates to meet critical operational needs.

Employees are expected to take at least one period of ten consecutive working
days each year, unless local practice or personal circumstances make this
impractical.

## 6. Carryover

Up to five unused vacation days may be carried into the next vacation year.
Carried days must be used by March 31 and are used before the new year's
entitlement.

Unused days above the five-day limit expire at year-end unless:

- the employee could not take leave because of certified long-term sickness,
  protected leave, or a documented company requirement; or
- local law requires a longer carryover period.

Managers must not repeatedly prevent an employee from taking earned vacation.

## 7. Changes and cancellation

An employee may change or cancel approved vacation with the manager's consent.
The company may cancel approved vacation only for a serious and unforeseen
business need. In that case, the business unit will reimburse reasonable,
non-refundable personal travel costs supported by receipts.

## 8. Sickness during vacation

Vacation days may be converted to sick leave when the employee:

- notifies the manager as soon as reasonably possible;
- follows the normal sickness-reporting procedure; and
- provides a medical certificate within three calendar days where legally
  permitted.

Converted vacation days are returned to the employee's balance.

## 9. Pay and termination

Vacation is paid at the employee's normal base rate, including other elements
required by local law. The company does not normally pay cash instead of
vacation during employment.

On termination, unused accrued vacation is paid or scheduled during the notice
period as permitted by law. Vacation taken beyond the accrued balance may be
deducted from final pay where legally permitted and authorized.

## 10. Responsibilities

Employees must maintain an accurate leave record, plan handovers, and set an
out-of-office message. Managers must apply this directive consistently, protect
team coverage, and encourage employees to use their entitlement.

People Operations maintains leave balances and advises on statutory or
exceptional cases.

## 11. Non-compliance and exceptions

Absence without prior approval may be treated as unauthorized absence.
Exceptions require written approval from the People Operations Director.
