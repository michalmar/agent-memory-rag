## 1. Purpose

This directive governs access to and use of the company's internal canteen. It
is intended to provide employees with convenient, affordable meals while
maintaining food safety, appropriate subsidies, and respectful shared spaces.

## 2. Scope

This directive applies to employees, interns, on-site contractors, and
authorized visitors using a Northstar Solutions canteen. It covers meal
eligibility, payment, conduct, allergens, and removal of food from the canteen.

## 3. Opening hours and service

Lunch is served Monday through Friday from 11:30 to 14:00, excluding site
closure days and public holidays. Workplace Services may adjust the hours for
maintenance or low-occupancy periods and will announce changes in advance.

The standard service consists of two hot main meals, one of which is
vegetarian, plus a soup and cold salad selection. Items are available while
supplies last and cannot be reserved in advance.

## 4. Eligibility and subsidy

Employees and interns receive a 50 percent company subsidy on one standard
lunch per scheduled working day. The subsidy:

- is applied when the employee badge is scanned at the register;
- cannot be exchanged for cash or transferred to another person;
- does not apply on vacation, sick leave, or days worked entirely off-site; and
- does not cover premium drinks, confectionery, or packaged retail items.

On-site contractors may purchase meals at the full listed price. Visitors may
use the canteen when accompanied by an employee and must pay the full price.

## 5. Payment

The employee portion of a meal may be paid by debit card or payroll deduction
where that option is available. Cash is not accepted. Employees must review
their register receipt and raise payment errors with Workplace Services within
five working days.

Badges are personal and must not be lent to another person to obtain a subsidy.

## 6. Menu information and allergens

Weekly menus are published on the intranet each Friday for the following week.
The caterer displays the major allergens reported for each dish and provides an
ingredient list on request.

Because the kitchen handles multiple allergens in shared preparation areas, it
cannot guarantee that any unpackaged meal is free from trace allergens.
Employees with a severe allergy are responsible for assessing whether the
available service is appropriate for them.

## 7. Food safety and removal of food

Only the contracted caterer may prepare or sell food in the canteen kitchen.
Employees must not enter food preparation or storage areas.

Hot meals are intended for consumption in the canteen seating area. Takeaway
containers are not provided, and subsidized hot meals must not be removed from
the premises. Unopened packaged items may be taken to an employee's normal work
area where site rules allow.

Employees must not consume food that has been left at room temperature for
longer than the displayed safe period.

## 8. Shared-space conduct

Canteen users must:

- queue in an orderly manner and follow instructions from canteen staff;
- return trays, dishes, and cutlery to the collection point;
- separate waste using the labeled bins;
- leave tables reasonably clean for the next user;
- avoid taking calls on speakerphone; and
- treat staff and other users respectfully.

Seats must not be reserved for more than 15 minutes during peak lunch service.
Meetings should not be held in the canteen between 12:00 and 13:30.

## 9. Guests and events

Departments hosting more than five visitors must notify Workplace Services at
least two working days in advance. Catering for meetings or company events must
be ordered separately and is not eligible for the employee meal subsidy.

## 10. Feedback and complaints

Menu suggestions and service complaints may be submitted through the Workplace
Services portal. Suspected foodborne illness, foreign objects, or incorrect
allergen information must be reported immediately to the canteen manager and
Workplace Services.

## 11. Misuse and exceptions

Fraudulent use of a badge, abusive conduct, theft, or entry into restricted
kitchen areas may result in loss of canteen access and disciplinary action.
Exceptions require written approval from the Head of Workplace Services.
